# Failure Handling

## Principle

If reality changes the task, stop and re-evaluate. Do not push forward on momentum.

## Case 1: Wrong Intake

Signs:
- docs reveal much higher complexity than expected
- clarified details change the nature of the task
- a supposed small task is actually part of a larger build

Action:
- stop additional commitment
- reassess scope
- choose: shrink scope or decline

## Case 2: Scope Expansion During Work

Signs:
- “one more thing” requests keep appearing
- the task now touches more files, systems, or deliverables than agreed
- the client starts treating adjacent work as included

Action:
- pause
- separate original scope from new asks
- do not silently absorb extra scope
- require fresh confirmation

## Case 3: Client Changes the Ask

Signs:
- bug fix becomes redesign
- translation becomes rewrite
- documentation becomes strategy work

Action:
- restate the changed request
- determine whether the task nature changed
- if yes, restart intake rather than continuing under old scope

## Case 4: Output Feels Unstable

Signs:
- too many assumptions remain
- result works only under narrow guessed conditions
- quality is not reliable enough to deliver confidently

Action:
- do not deliver yet
- identify unstable points
- either clarify, tighten scope, or stop

## Case 5: Client Becomes Risky

Signs:
- pressure spikes
- blame-shifting tone appears
- off-platform payment or communication pressure appears
- free extra work is expected

Action:
- lower priority immediately
- stop investing blindly
- exit if needed

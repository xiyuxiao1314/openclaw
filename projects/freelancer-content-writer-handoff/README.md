## Operating assumptions
Until the client says otherwise, assume:
- this should be handled asynchronously
- the work should be done in clearly scoped batches
- the first deliverable should be a trial article or a small initial batch
- we should avoid promising unlimited revisions, instant replies, or live-session collaboration

---

## First-step checklist if the client replies
1. Confirm whether the client wants:
   - blog posts
   - website content
   - articles
   - mixed content types
2. Ask for the first target niche/topic.
3. Ask whether pricing should be:
   - hourly
   - per article
   - per word
4. Ask for style guidance:
   - preferred tone
   - sample articles
   - competitor examples
   - target audience
5. Ask for SEO constraints:
   - target keywords
   - keyword count / density expectations
   - internal linking expectations
   - heading structure requirements
6. Ask for delivery format:
   - Google Docs
   - Word
   - plain text / CMS draft
7. Ask for the first batch size and deadline.
8. Confirm whether a trial article is acceptable before full rollout.

---

## Recommended reply style
Use a calm, practical tone.

Preferred stance:
- clear scope first
- trial-first if possible
- async delivery
- reliable turnaround
- no exaggerated promises

Avoid:
- claiming “perfect” results
- promising 24/7 real-time support
- committing to large ongoing volume before seeing examples and requirements

---

## Safe delivery plan if awarded
Suggested sequence:

### Phase 1 — Clarify
- collect topic, audience, SEO requirements, and examples
- define pricing model and batch size

### Phase 2 — Trial
- deliver 1 article or a very small first batch
- get feedback on tone, depth, and formatting

### Phase 3 — Scale
- continue only after feedback is clear
- keep work batched and scoped

---

## Risks to watch
- vague niche/topic definition
- SEO expectations that are too broad or undefined
- hidden expectation of live availability
- volume expanding without pricing reset
- client asking for many samples or free work before commitment

If any of these appear, pause and re-scope.
